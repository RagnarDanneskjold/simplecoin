I've been mining for [x] hours and haven't gotten paid yet. What's up?

Simple Doge is a PPLNS pool which means that once a block is solved by someone in the pool it gets distributed among the workers who helped to solve it. Finding blocks is random, just like rolling a dice, so sometimes it takes longer than others to solve a block. Really large pools have more people working to solve blocks, so they find them faster. But this also means that you get paid out less for each solved block. Over time mining with a large pool or a small pool will come out to the same amount of money, but since small pools solve blocks less frequently it can take longer for it all to average out.

What do I enter for my worker name?

It doesn't matter, it can be anything you choose as long as it doesn't contain spaces or unicode character. Different worker names will allow you to easily identify each machine connected to our pool on your User Stats page.

What does round luck mean?

Round luck is how lucky it would be to solve a block right now. The longer it has taken the less lucky we are, so the percentage goes down. 100% is the expected average luck over time. 200% means it was found twice as fast while 50% means it took twice as long as hoped.
